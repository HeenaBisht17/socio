A voice for the voiceless, a symbol of courage and education:

Malala Yousafzai

Early Life

Born on July 12, 1997, in Mingora, Swat Valley, Pakistan. Grew up in a family that valued education; her father, Ziauddin Yousafzai, ran a school and was an education activist.

Activism Begins Early

Started blogging for BBC Urdu at the age of 11, under a pseudonym, about life under Taliban rule and the importance of education for girls.

Taliban Attack and Survival

On October 9, 2012, Malala was shot in the head by a Taliban gunman while riding a school bus. She survived after being airlifted to the UK for treatment, becoming a global figure overnight.
